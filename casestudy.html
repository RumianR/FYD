Our Case Study

We dived into the most fundemental aspects required to create the best user experience possible.

Clear understanding the problem

In today’s day and age data is everything. Major Fortune 500 companies are trying everything in their power to obtain our data in subtle ways without giving us much clear control over it. Companies such as Google and Facebook use this data to run successful ad campaigns and target a specific audience. Their success allows them to partner with more advertisers and generate more revenue for themselves without rewarding the user of anything. Users feel like they lack control and do not benefit from the sharing of their data.

Why selling data is challenging

According to contributor at TNW, attempts at selling individual’s data fail to overcome two major problems.

First — you need to collect all of your personal data to be able to sell it for more than few pennies. That means huge data sets, terabytes of data. Doing this yourself is a daunting and expensive task, accompanied by huge security risks (consider what happens if such data set is mishandled). You may end up spending more time and effort collecting, safekeeping, and transferring your data, than earning from the sale.

Finally - the lack of efficient data market. There has to be transparent and competitive market for price discovery. Moreover, the sellers’ and buyers’ bargaining powers have to be about equal. There have to be many buyers, and individual sellers have to be somehow “unionized.”

Our Solution

Our team at Dezignz has been contracted by FreeYourData to provide a mobile app solution to users who want more control and benefits over their data. The solution consists of creating a simple and elegant app for users to easily sell their data. There will be a central marketplace which accepts different types of user data. It can range from anything such as fitness, health, financial and social media data. This solution allows the user to choose what data they want to share, for how long they want to share and they also make money by doing so. This should drive big companies to respect users data and provide them the control and benefits they deserve.
